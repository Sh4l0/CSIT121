The standard is used most by automated services and other speech recognition software. It is also applicable to other services that need to understand and complete actions based on what is said. SRGS has various use cases. However, I want to highlight my favorite 2.


One use case is a Semantic Interpreter. This means it’s able to understand and act upon the underlying meaning of a phrase (I want a flight= book flight). This proves especially useful when a company needs to automate a call line which interacts with customers. Another example may be online assistants such as Siri and Alexa.


Another is allowing for alternative meanings for phrases. This means that the SRS is able to take in a variety of terms and phrases as a signal to complete a specific action. For example, understanding a negative response from the phrase “no”, “stop”, “cancel”, etc. This helps improve the usage experience and the reactivity of the system.
